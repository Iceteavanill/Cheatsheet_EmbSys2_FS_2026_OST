\section{Embedded Memory}

\subsection{Memory Types}
% GOAL V05 • You are familiar with the basic types of embedded memory.
  \vspace{-0.1cm}
  \begin{center}
    \begin{tabular}{llll}
      \toprule
      \textbf{Type} & \textbf{Volatile} & \textbf{Writable}   & \textbf{Typical Use} \\ \midrule
      SRAM          & yes               & yes                 & Cache, Stack, Heap, Variables \\
      DRAM          & yes               & yes                 & Main memory (typ.\ external) \\
      ROM           & no                & no                  & Boot code, Constants \\
      Flash         & no                & yes (block/sector erase) & Program code, Persistent data \\
      EEPROM        & no                & yes (byte-wise write)    & Configuration, Calibration data \\
      \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
  \end{center}
  \vspace{-0.1cm}
  \textit{SRAM is fast, requires no refresh; DRAM is denser, cheaper, and requires periodic refresh.}

\subsection{Memory Model and Memory Map}
% GOAL V05 • You are familiar with the memory model and memory map as typically used in a microcontroller.
% GOAL L08 • You know the memory model of a microcontroller and how to find the different memory locations.

  \begin{minipage}[t]{0.58\linewidth}
    Memory locations are determined from the microcontroller reference manual (Memory Map chapter) or development tools (e.g. STM32CubeIDE linker map / build analyzer).
  
    Cortex-M systems use a flat 32-bit address space ($2^{32} = \qty{4}{GiB}$) with memory-mapped Flash, RAM, and peripheral regions.
    External memories (e.g. QSPI Flash) are also mapped into the address space via the memory map.
  \end{minipage}\hfill

      \textbf{Section} & \textbf{Location} & \textbf{Content} \\ \midrule
      \texttt{.text}   & Flash & Code, constants (\texttt{const}) \\
      \texttt{.rodata} & Flash & Read-only data \\
      \texttt{.data}   & RAM (init from Flash) & Initialized global/static variables \\
      \texttt{.bss}    & RAM & Zero-initialized global/static variables \\
      Heap             & RAM $\uparrow$ & Dynamic allocation (\texttt{new}/\texttt{pvPortMalloc}) \\
