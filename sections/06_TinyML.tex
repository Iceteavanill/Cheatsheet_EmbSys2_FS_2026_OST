\subsection{Deep Neural Network}
% GOAL V12 • You can explain the basic structure and functioning of a deep learning model (fully connected deep neural network, input/hidden/output layers, activation functions, biases and weights).
Layers: \textbf{input} (features), \textbf{hidden} (neurons), 
\textbf{output} (classes/values), 
plus \textbf{bias}. \emph{Depth} $=$ \#layers $-1$; 
\emph{weight size} between layers $M,M{+}1$ $=$ neurons$_M\cdot$neurons$_{M+1}$. 
A \textbf{weight} $w$ scales an input, a \textbf{bias} $b$ offsets the output.
% \[ z = \sum_i w_i x_i + b = \mathbf{W}^T\mathbf{x}, \qquad y = f(z). \]

\subsubsection{Propagation} 
\textbf{Forward} (inference): $z^{[l]}=\mathbf{W}^{[l]T}\mathbf{x}+\mathbf{b}^{[l]}$, $a^{[l]}=g(z^{[l]})$. 
\textbf{Backward} (training): adjust weights/biases from the error.

\begin{minipage}{0.4\columnwidth}
    \textbf{Overfit} (high variance): too complex / too little data \textrightarrow{} large train--test gap. 
    \textbf{Underfit} (high bias): too simple \textrightarrow{} high training error. 
    \textbf{Optimum:} low train \emph{and} test error (regularization helps).
\end{minipage}

\subsection{Model Optimization}
% GOAL V13 • You can explain why and how model optimization leads to performance improvements, which are particularly important for TinyML.
Optimization is driven by hardware bounds (Roofline) and parallelism limits (Amdahl/Gustafson).
\example{FCNN cost} 
10 inputs, 20 hidden, 
5 outputs, 
8 B/value: weights $200+100=300$, 
biases $20+5=25$ \textrightarrow{} $325$ params $=2600$\,B $\approx2.54$\,kB (fits a Cortex-M4 easily); 
MACs $=N_\text{in}\cdot N_\text{out}=300$.
\begin{ctabular}{ll}
\textbf{Model optimization} & \textbf{Implementation optimization} \\ \hline
quantization & operator fusion \\
pruning & SIMD/vectorization \\
distillation & CMSIS-NN \\
NAS & DMA, NPU offloading \\
\end{ctabular}

\subsubsection{Quantization / Compression} 
% GOAL V13 • You can distinguish different number formats and quantization method types used in TinyML training and inference.
Reduces numerical precision of weights/activations
(\texttt{float32}\textrightarrow{}\texttt{int8} $=4\times$ smaller) with minimal accuracy loss. 
Formats: FP32/FP16/BF16 (BF16 keeps FP32's 8-bit exponent, trades mantissa), INT/Q-format fixed point.(BF=Brain floatingpoint)
\begin{outline}
  \1 \textbf{Symmetric:} $x_q=\text{round}(x/s)$. \textbf{Asymmetric (affine):} $x_q=\text{round}(x/s+z)$ with scale $s$ and zero-point $z$ mapping real $0$ to an integer.
  \1 \textbf{Weights} are static; \textbf{activations} are dynamic and need calibration.
  \1 \textbf{Clipping} saturates outliers to improve resolution; scale granularity can be layer-/channel-/block-wise.
\end{outline}

\subsubsection{Pruning}
removes redundant parameters \textrightarrow{} \textbf{sparse} matrices. 
\textbf{Structured} (whole rows/channels: hardware-efficient, lower max sparsity) vs.
\textbf{unstructured magnitude} pruning (individual smallest-$|w|$ weights: highest sparsity but needs sparse-matrix support).

\subsubsection{Distillation \& NAS(Neural Architecture Search)} 
% GOAL V13 • You can describe what quantization, pruning, knowledge distillation, and neural architecture search (NAS) conceptionally do with a neural network.
\textbf{Knowledge distillation:} a small \emph{student model} mimics a large \emph{teacher model} via \emph{soft labels} (softmax with temperature $T$).

\textbf{NAS:} \say{learning model architecture and parameters together}
automated search over a space with a search strategy $+$ performance estimator (one-shot learns architecture $+$ weights jointly).

\subsubsection{Metrics} 
memory, latency, energy, accuracy, F1. From the confusion matrix: accuracy $\frac{TP+TN}{\text{all}}$, precision $\frac{TP}{TP+FP}$, recall $\frac{TP}{TP+FN}$,
\[ F_1 = 2\,\frac{\text{precision}\cdot\text{recall}}{\text{precision}+\text{recall}} = \frac{2TP}{2TP+FP+FN}. \]

\subsection{Convolutional Neural Networks}
% GOAL V14 • You can contextualize the CNN network within the entire deep learning workflow, including preprocessing, post processing and its use in an embedded vision application and other typical application scenarios.
% GOAL V14 • You can explain the basic architecture of a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN)with its characteristic layers(conv, padding, pool, flatten).
CNN's are deep learning networks that are designed for grid structured data like pictures.
\textbf{Key Idea:} Constrain the architecture to reduce the number of parameters by exploiting image properties:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Hidden layers stay \textbf{locally connected} (limited receptive field).
  \item \textbf{Weight sharing} between hidden units (same kernel scanned over input).
  \item Hidden layers may be \textbf{subsampled} (pooling).
\end{itemize}
